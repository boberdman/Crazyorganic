Fungi are their own kingdom of life — closer to us than to plants — and most of their species have never even been named. Beneath a single forest floor runs a web of fungal threads that trades sugar, water, and maybe something stranger between trees. And at the far edge of all this sit the questions that keep us up at night: Can something think without a brain? What is consciousness, really? Where does one living thing end and another begin?

Crazy Organic chases those questions — the wondrous ones and the unanswered ones — and brings you along for the chase.

Our deal with you

We ask big, bold questions — bigger than careful science writing usually risks. But we never dress a guess up as a fact to make the story land.

When something is established, we'll say so plainly. When it's contested, we'll show you both camps. When it's a gorgeous, unprovable idea, we'll call it that — and enjoy it anyway. The wonder is real precisely because the honesty is.

“We don't know yet” is one of our favorite sentences.
