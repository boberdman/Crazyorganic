## A · TOPIC BRIEF (Research → Content)

**Pillar:** 4 — What's real vs. what's sold (grounding)
**Working title:** Lion's Mane and the Memory Question
**Big question / hook:** Lion's mane is sold for focus and memory on one genuinely thrilling idea — that it makes nerve cells grow. The mechanism is real science. How much of it has actually been shown in *people*? (Answer: a small, short, mixed human evidence base that runs far behind the marketing — the cleanest "real vs. sold" case in the cabinet.)
**Key points:**
1. What's sold: lion's mane is the headliner nootropic mushroom (coffee/gummies/tinctures), sold for cognition; under DSHEA it can say "supports focus" but not treat/cure/prevent; FDA disclaimer required. [established re: regulation]
2. The idea: *Hericium erinaceus* contains hericenones (fruiting body) + erinacines (mycelium) that stimulate nerve growth factor (NGF) in vitro; erinacine A crosses the blood-brain barrier in animals. A real, exciting mechanism — on the preclinical rung. [preliminary]
3. The mouse story: in Alzheimer's-model mice, lion's mane / erinacine A reduces amyloid plaques, increases hippocampal neurogenesis, lowers neuroinflammation, improves memory tasks; healthy mice show ↑ hippocampal NGF activity. Strong — but it's mice. [preliminary]
4. The human trials, honestly: only a handful, small (n≈26–77), short, heterogeneous preparations/doses, mixed results. Mori 2009 (n=30 MCI) improved on HDS-R *during use*, faded after a 4-wk washout — the key "temporary, not rebuilt" tell. Saitsu 2019 (n=31 healthy ≥50) improved MMSE only (an odd instrument for healthy people), not two other memory tests. Li 2020 (n=41 early AD, erinacine-A mycelium, 1 yr) improved MMSE/IADL vs placebo (small, pilot-grade). [preliminary]
5. The acute "feel-it-today" studies genuinely disagree: some (La Monica 2023; Docherty 2023) report faster speed-of-performance / better mood / lower subjective stress; a more recent better-powered acute trial (Front. Nutr. 2025) found no significant overall benefit. [contested]
6. Honesty payoff: no large, long-term human trial shows a memory benefit in ordinary people; the headline NGF jump has **never been measured in a living human brain** (trials infer it from cognitive scores). Plus the cross-aisle problem — preparation heterogeneity (hericenones≠erinacines; fruiting body vs mycelium-on-grain) means store products may not match trial extracts (ADDF flags this explicitly). [established that it's unproven/heterogeneous]
7. Safety: usually mild (GI, headache), balanced vs placebo; but documented allergic reactions incl. ≥1 serious allergic lung-reaction case report; long-term safety data thin. [established]
**Epistemic status:** a deliberately **Preliminary-and-Contested** piece — exactly the rung-exercise the Topic Ledger wants Pillar 4 doing. Mechanism = Preliminary; mouse data = Preliminary; human cognition = Preliminary; acute effects = Contested; regulation + "mechanism unmeasured in humans" + safety-edge = Established. No Established *efficacy* claim is made.
**Sources to use:** FDA Structure/Function (DSHEA); ADDF Cognitive Vitality "Lion's Mane" review (read in full); Mori et al. 2009 *Phytother Res* (corroborated 2×); 2025 PRISMA systematic review (PMC12434001, PROSPERO CRD42024571250) — RCT table + safety; Li et al. 2020 *Front Aging Neurosci*; Docherty et al. 2023 *Nutrients* (acute/chronic young adults); 2025 *Front Nutr* acute null trial.
**Claims-risk flag:** **HEALTH-CLAIM → ESCALATE (permanent §5 gate).** Series B / Theme D is permanently Tier 1 (Config §4 #3, §5; Ledger hard-line register). Handling notes carried to Content: (a) never assert efficacy — frame every benefit as small/short/mixed/preliminary; (b) keep mouse results visibly fenced as mouse results; (c) no dosing framed as instruction and no "take this" nudge — report only; (d) keep the "not medical advice / talk to a clinician" beat; (e) carry the safety/allergy flag without alarmism.

3. *(the honest turn)* So what happened in people? A handful of small, short trials that don't agree. The most-cited (30 people) improved — then the gain *faded once they stopped*. The newest, better-run quick-effect trial found basically nothing. No big long-term study exists, and the famous "nerve growth" has never been measured in a living human brain. Beautiful mechanism, unfinished human story. Worth watching — not "clinically proven." Not medical advice. — *Preliminary / acute effects Contested*
