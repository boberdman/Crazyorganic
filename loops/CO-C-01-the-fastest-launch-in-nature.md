## A · TOPIC BRIEF (Research → Content)

**Pillar:** 1 — The unexplored kingdom (wonder)
**Working title:** The Fastest Launch in Nature
**Big question / hook:** The most violent movement in the living world isn't a mantis or a trap-jaw ant — it's a fungus the size of an eyelash, growing on dung, firing its spores at the sun.
**Key points:**
1. *Pilobolus* ("hat thrower") is coprophilous; its cycle requires being eaten by a grazer, so it must fling spores off the dung onto clean grass. [established]
2. Mechanism: a pressurised subsporangial vesicle ruptures and launches the spore cap; a sticky ring adheres it to vegetation. [established]
3. Numbers (Yafetto et al. 2008, filmed up to 250,000 fps): up to ~25 m/s (~90 km/h), accelerations up to ~180,000 g — "fastest flights in nature" by acceleration. [established]
4. Phototropic — aims at light; the vesicle acts as a crude lens. [established]
5. Honest correction: power is ordinary osmotic turgor, NOT exotic pressure — 2008 measurement corrected earlier model-based overestimates. [established]
**Epistemic status:** all **established** (pure-wonder Pillar-1 piece; appropriate early-year grounding).
**Sources:** Yafetto et al. 2008, PLoS ONE 3(9):e3237 (primary); Britannica "Pilobolus" (fact-checked); Wikipedia "Pilobolus" (secondary, lens detail).
**Claims-risk flag:** NONE. No health/legal claims; no §4 hard lines. Handling note: pin "fastest" to *acceleration*, not velocity — kill "faster than a bullet."
**Target formats:** Newsletter + 3 X posts.
