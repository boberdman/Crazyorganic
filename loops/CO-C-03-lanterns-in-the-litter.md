## A · TOPIC BRIEF (Research → Content)

**Pillar:** 1 — The unexplored kingdom (wonder)
**Working title:** Lanterns in the Litter
**Big question / hook:** Some rotting logs really do glow in the dark with a soft green light — living, heatless "foxfire." The light is real and, as of 2018, the chemistry is fully solved. The honest twist is that we still can't fully say *why* the fungi do it.
**Key points:**
1. Foxfire is genuine fungal bioluminescence in decaying wood — cold light, documented since Aristotle (~382 BC) and Pliny; folk-named "fairy fire"; used to light the compass of the Revolutionary-War submarine *Turtle*; traced to fungi in 1823. [established; lore beats = folklore]
2. It's rare and scattered: ~130 known bioluminescent fungal species (roughly double the count of ~15 years ago), across several separate Agaricales lineages (*Armillaria*, *Omphalotus*, *Panellus stipticus*, etc.); nearly all emit green light peaking ~520–530 nm. [established]
3. Mechanism: a luciferin (3-hydroxyhispidin, built from caffeic acid) + luciferase; the *complete* biosynthetic cycle was characterized in 2018 — the first fully described bioluminescence pathway in any eukaryote — and was transplanted into yeast and plants to make them glow. [established]
4. Function (best case): in *Neonothopanus gardneri* (Brazil, *flor-de-coco*), the glow is circadian-clock-controlled and peaks at night; LED-lit fake mushrooms attracted beetles/flies/wasps/ants far more than dark controls → evidence the light recruits spore-dispersing insects. [established for that species]
5. Honest payoff: that function is NOT universal. The ghost fungus *Omphalotus nidiformis* glow attracted no extra insects; many fungi glow mainly in buried mycelium (no night sky, few flying insects). So "glows to attract spore-carriers" is well-supported in one species and genuinely unsettled as a general rule — may vary by species, or in some be an incidental metabolic by-product. [contested]
**Epistemic status:** mostly **established** (wonder rendered through solved chemistry + documented history), with the **function/"why"** explicitly **contested** — a clean live-science beat, balancing the established-dominant Series C run without drifting into speculation.

Fungi* (diversity review — species count, lineages, wavelength); Kotlobay et al. 2018 *PNAS* (pathway, glowing yeast/plants); Oliveira et al. 2015 *Current Biology* (circadian + insect attraction); Weinstein, Delean, Wood & Austin 2016 *IMA Fungus* (ghost-fungus null result + by-product hypothesis).
